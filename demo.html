Verified is ledger revenue as a share of that platform's own claimed revenue for the same 28 days. The unverified remainder is a mix of platform double counting and orders server-side attribution can't see.

How this example's claimed numbers are made: the simulated store has a known true contribution per channel, and each platform's claimed revenue is that true contribution inflated by a platform-typical factor, plus partial credit for organic sales the channel did not drive. Those are the same two mechanisms that make real dashboards add up to more than a real store sold. The ledger side counts the simulated orders themselves, resolved to channels by the same evidence rules the product applies to real orders. On a live account nothing is simulated: claimed numbers come straight from each platform's reporting API, and the ledger comes from your Shopify orders.
